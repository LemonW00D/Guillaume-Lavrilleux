About Me

Hello! I’m Guillaume, a sound designer based in Bordeaux, France. I specialize in video games and interactive experiences. Former musician, it is thanks to the use of synthesizers that I ended up getting interested more in sound textures than in music.

I started by participating in game jams and then spent 2 years working on VR audio at Lucine in Bordeaux. It allowed me to work with 2D artists, 3D modelers, UX designers, UI designers, and various programmers : from gameplay prog, technical artists, to machine learning developers. It helped a lot to improve my work, and also confirmed that every aspect of creating a video game, or an interactive experience will have an impact on the audio.

Designing audio environments is like telling a story. I try to make people feel emotions through sounds, while also giving a strong knowledge of what’s going on in their experience. Making sure they receive the right feedback at the right time.

Working on those aspects require creative and technical skills, and I like to express them with tools such as Reaper and Ableton Live. On the technical part, I can use both Fmod and Wwise, before getting my hands into game engines. I also do a bit of coding so I can smoothly work with fellow programmers.

Beside that I am also part of a game audio collective that I co-founded with fellow sound designers which is called Game Audio France (GAFR, pun intended). We started as 4 people early 2017 and we are now one of the biggest french game audio collective doing monthly meet up and sharing knowledge. I am also a modular synthesizer and tea enthusiast!

Works

Lucine aims to help people who suffer from chronic pain with audio and visual stimulations. My work at Lucine consisted of designing audio environment for different VR platforms: Samsung Gear, HTC Vive, Oculus Rift and Oculus Quest. I had to handle the audio artistic direction in collaboration with the visual artists. I also had the chance to work with researchers (neuroscientists, anthropologist, psychologists) to target specific audio stimulations to use.

Défi Curieux! is an interactive experience that took place in Bordeaux, France, at Cap Sciences. The experience is a mix between a TV show and a mobile app. Users interact through a mobile phone to play a competitive game. The game needed musical ambient loops, jingles and UI SFX to make it feel more immersive.
